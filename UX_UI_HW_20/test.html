The Informed Traveler

The Informed Traveler allows travelers to create a travel plan from authentic and local-approved travel destinations.

Timeline: August - October 2020

Role: User Research, UX

Introduction

As my first project into the world of UX, I tackled designing a travel planning mobile app.

The Problem

I observed that travelers were having trouble and spending too much time picking unique locations to visit for their travels. These locations tended to be overpriced and the same basic tourist experience recommended by almost every travelling website. How might I create a travel planning app so that travelers can easily and quickly find authentic experiences that fit within their budget and vacation time?

The Solution

Create The Informed Traveler, a travel planning experience, that allows the user to choose locations based on local and traveler reviews.

User Research

I found 5 users that fit my requirements to be interviewed: people who had traveled and participated in creating travel plans at least once. My goals in interviewing these 5 people were to:

  • Which methods did they enjoy using to plan their travels?
  • What were the most frustrating parts of their travel planning processes?

Based on my findings in the diagram below, my interviewees expressed a need for visiting unique, local recommended places in order to avoid popular tourist attractions that were often over hyped.

Persona: Miriam Garcia

I want you to meet Miriam Garcia, a sharp and highly individualistic traveler, who was born from the interviews. Miriam is sick of searching for unique travel destinations only to find overpriced tourist traps. It's time for her to find an authentic traveling experience.

Ideation

Our problem remains as stated under "The Problem" section. I began thinking of possible features to include in the traveling app by thinking of Miriam and what she would want to see in a travel app.

Since this is my first project and I only had so much time to work on this app, I prioritized which features I thought would be the most helpful to Miriam while still being easy to implement.

I envisioned the journey that Miriam would take through the app in the journey map below.

Prototyping

After several revisions, I finally translated Miriam's journey through the app in a user flow that would allow me to understand how to plan the layout of The Informed Traveler.

Digital Wireframes

I quickly created digital wireframes of the paper sketches I had made of the app.

Testing

Guerilla Testing

I tested my wireframes and evaluated what was working and not working for the app so far.

I concluded that users had trouble distinguishing local reviews from traveler reviews and that the filter button was not easily distinguishable.

I made iterations to the user flow based on these suggestions and proceded to make the final iOS prototype due to time constraints.

Prototyping Round 2

Finally, I created a clickable hi-fi prototype designed for iOS based on the feedback from Guerilla testing the low-fi prototype I had made.

Results

My takeaway from this experience is to not get caught up in feature creep. I feel like I have spent a lot of time thinking about all the features that I could include in the app when I had already narrowed down that list based on feedback from user interviews and user testing. While making prototypes, I kept thinking of more features to place into the app, but I needed to focus more on the features prioritized.
For future projects, I will focus more on prioritized features. I will also try to remember to iterate as early and often as I can so that I do not get too caught up in one idea for a project.
